Help & Tutorial

What is NucleoSpec?

NucleoSpec is a web-based tool for assigning compositions to negative-mode ESI mass spectra of nucleic acid–metal complexes.

Given a spectrum and a nucleic acid sequence (or formula), NucleoSpec will:

  • Auto-detect charge states from isotope spacing
  • Search for matching compositions (strand count, metal count, N0, Qcl)
  • Generate theoretical isotope patterns for visual overlay comparison
  • Handle adducts (Na+, NH4+, Cl−, custom) automatically

Supports DNA-AgN, complex DNA/XNA, and custom XNA (TNA, PNA, LNA, etc.) modes.

Upload Data

  • This tool is designed for negative mode ESI-MS data
  • Supported formats: .txt or .csv
  • Two columns: m/z (mass-to-charge) and intensity
  • Tab or space separated
  • Example: 
    500.123    1000
500.624    850
501.125    600
  • Drag & drop file or click "Select File"
  • Resolution is auto-detected from your spectrum

Choose Analysis Mode

Three modes available:

DNA-AgN Mode
  • For single-stranded DNA with silver nanoclusters
  • Enter DNA sequence 5'→3' (e.g., CACCTAGCGA)
  • Standard bases only: A, T, G, C
  • Click "Apply DNA Settings" to confirm
Ag(I)-DNA/XNA Complex Mode
  • For double-stranded DNA or DNA/XNA hybrids
  • Enter both strands 5'→3'
  • Select metal to search (Ag, Na, K, Mg, etc.)
  • Optional: Use custom XNA formulas for non-DNA strands
  • Click "Apply Complex Settings" to confirm
Custom XNA Mode
  • For non-standard nucleotides (TNA, PNA, LNA, etc.)
  • Enter XNA name and chemical formula per nucleotide
  • Click "Apply XNA Settings" to confirm

Analyze Peaks

  • Click any peak in the spectrum to analyze it
  • Charge state (z) is auto-detected based on isotope spacing
  • Color-coded markers indicate charge state (see legend on spectrum)
  • Small peaks (< 1% intensity) may not show markers but can still be clicked
  • Zoom in to double-check the charge state assignment
  • If the Gaussian fit looks poor, use manual fitting to adjust
  • Composition matches appear in the "Composition Analysis" section below

Understanding Results

Each composition result shows these key parameters:

z — Charge state of the ion (determined from isotope spacing = 1/z)
nAg — Total number of silver atoms (nAg = N0 + Qcl)
N0 — Number of valence (neutral) electrons on the silver cluster. Stable clusters typically have even N0 (2, 4, 6, ...)
Qcl — Charge of the inorganic core. Satisfies: N0 + Qcl = nAg
ΔX0 — Difference between theoretical and experimental centroid (m/z). Lower is better; < 0.1 m/z is a strong match. Primary criterion for Best Fit selection.
Pattern similarity — Mean of cosine similarity and Pearson correlation between experimental and theoretical isotope envelopes (0–1). Reported as a confidence indicator, not the ranking metric. Tiers: ▲ > 0.8 high, ○ 0.5–0.8 moderate, ▽ < 0.5 low
The composition marked Best Fit has the lowest ΔX0 (best centroid match). Pattern similarity and the isotope-pattern overlay serve as visual confirmation of the assignment

Overlay & Compare

  • Check the box next to any composition to overlay its theoretical isotope pattern
  • The overlay appears in the zoom plot on a secondary y-axis
  • A good match: theoretical peaks align with experimental peaks in m/z position
  • A poor match: peaks are shifted or the envelope shape doesn't match
  • Multiple overlays can be shown at once to compare candidates
  • If the best fit overlay looks offset, try the neighboring N0 values

Advanced Features

Re-analyze & Manual Fit
  • Re-analyze with new z: Override auto-detected charge state
  • Manual Gaussian fit: Click two points on the peak to define the fitting range
  • Custom search: Manually specify strand count, nAg, z, and adduct
Custom Adducts
  • Click "Manage Adducts" to add custom adducts
  • Built-in adducts: NH4+, Na+, Cl− (always included)
  • Enter name, formula (or mass), and charge
  • Multiples (1x, 2x) are auto-generated
  • Check "Prioritize" for conjugating compounds
Draw Structure (JSME)
  • Click "Draw Structure" to open molecule editor
  • Draw molecule to get its chemical formula
  • Useful for conjugates or modifications

Tips & Troubleshooting

  • No peaks detected? Check file format – ensure two columns (m/z, intensity)
  • Poor matches? Try manual Gaussian fitting or custom composition search
  • Wrong charge state? Zoom in to verify isotope spacing, then re-analyze with correct z
  • Overlay looks offset? The neighboring N0 value may be a better match – check by overlaying it
  • Odd N0 warning? Stable DNA-AgN clusters typically have even N0; odd values may indicate a calibration issue
  • Start fresh? Click "Reset Analysis" to clear all data
  • Data privacy: Uploaded spectra are processed on the server but are not stored – all data is discarded after analysis
